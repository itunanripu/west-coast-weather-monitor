## Atmospheric River Summary (Wikipedia)
An atmospheric river (AR) is a narrow corridor or filament of enhanced water vapor transport in the lower atmosphere, typically 500‑1000 km wide and several thousand km long, resembling a river of water vapor in satellite imagery. These phenomena are responsible for 30‑50% of the total precipitation over the western United States and similar regions globally. Atmospheric rivers can deliver extreme rainfall, leading to flooding, landslides, and significant impacts on water resources. They are classified by intensity from AR 1 (weak) to AR 5 (exceptional).

## Recent News (Google Search)
In 2024, the West Coast experienced multiple atmospheric river events with record rainfall, causing widespread flooding, mudslides, and evacuations in California and Oregon. NOAA and UCAR reports highlighted increased intensity due to climate change, with AR 4 and AR 5 events contributing billions in damages. Research emphasizes improved forecasting to mitigate risks.
